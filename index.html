Overview

What is Cloud Storage?

Cloud storage is data held in a server, usually held by a company such as google or amazon, which is backed up remotely and made readily avaliable to users over a network. Cloud is a better instalation than bare metal installation due to the ability to transfer information quickly through the company and the ability to scale with the companies needs.

Private

Private cloud storage is privately hosted servers that are private to the user and company. These servers are mostly used in big business where said company has confidential data that is private to that company. While these servers are more protected than public they are still subject to breaches shown when Target lost millions of customer data to a malicious attack.

Public

Public data is most commonly know through providers like google with the google drive. These servers are most commonly used as of base storage for companies. This storage type while unprotected is effective in storing information due to the ability to "infinitley scale" to company needs, and distribute information company wide.

Cloud Providers

Amazon Web Services

Amazon Web Service (AWS) is one of the most protected public cloud providers. Companies such as GE, and Pintrest use AWS to host their data.

Microsoft Azure

Microsoft Azure, one of the most trusted cloud companies, with users such as BMW, Toyota, and Bank of America. Microsoft uses a cloud type called Hybrid, hybrid cloud is the mix between private and public cloud as the company needs, giving businesses a greter flexibility in the way they use their data.

Google Cloud Platform

Google Cloud Platform is another cloud provider for companies such as SnapChat and Spotify. GCP uses a hybrid cloud which gives you the ability to determine how you data is controlled.
